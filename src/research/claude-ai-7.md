## **User Question 7:**

### Claude's Response:

> What would your next steps be if you were thinking off moving to northern portugal or france?

Here's how I'd approach this systematically:

**Phase 1: Research & Reconnaissance (Next 3-6 months)**

**Financial Foundation:**

- Get pre-qualified for a mortgage in both countries (Portuguese banks vs French banks have different requirements)
- Research the tax implications of selling your New Hampshire property
- Calculate realistic budgets for property purchase, renovation, and 6-12 months operating expenses
- Look into Portugal's NHR tax scheme application process vs France's tax obligations for new residents

**Healthcare Transition Planning:**

- Contact your current gastroenterologist for detailed medical records and treatment history
- Research specific hospitals/gastroenterology centers in target regions (Porto area vs Languedoc)
- Understand the timeline for healthcare system registration in each country
- Investigate temporary health insurance options for the transition period

**Legal/Visa Requirements:**

- Confirm post-Brexit residence requirements for both countries
- Research business licensing requirements for gîte operations

**Phase 2: Location Scouting (6-12 months out)**

**Strategic Visits:**

- Plan 2-3 week trips to each country, staying in different areas
- Visit during different seasons if possible (summer heat vs winter accessibility)
- Stay in existing gîtes to understand the business from a guest perspective
- Meet with local estate agents, accountants, and other expat business owners

**Phase 3: Decision & Action (12-18 months)**

Would you prefer to start with the financial planning or take an extended scouting trip first? And are you thinking of keeping your New Hampshire property initially as a backup plan?
