> With the D7 one of the Personal Documents mentions medical certificates proving good health. Would that be a problem with the Crohn's?

This is an excellent and very important question. The good news is that having Crohn's disease should NOT disqualify you from the D7 visa, but you'll want to handle this carefully. Based on my research and understanding of immigration medical requirements, having Crohn's disease should NOT be a problem for your D7 visa application. Here's why:

**What "Medical Certificate of Good Health" Actually Means:**

The medical certificate requirement is primarily designed to screen for:

- **Communicable diseases** that pose public health risks (TB, HIV in some cases)
- **Mental health conditions** that might make you a danger to public safety
- **Drug addiction or severe substance abuse**
- **Conditions requiring immediate expensive medical intervention** that would burden the state

**Crohn's Disease Does NOT Fall Into These Categories:**

- It's not communicable
- It doesn't pose public safety risks
- It's a manageable chronic condition
- You have private health insurance coverage
- You're financially self-sufficient (key point!)

**How to Handle This Strategically:**

1. **Be Honest But Strategic**: Don't hide your condition, but frame it properly
    
2. **Get documentation from your gastroenterologist** showing:
    - Your condition is well-managed on Stelara
    - You're stable and not in active flare
    - You have ongoing medical care established
    - Your condition doesn't prevent normal daily activities
3. **Emphasize Your Self-Sufficiency**:
    - You have comprehensive health insurance
    - You have sufficient funds for ongoing medical care
    - You won't burden the Portuguese healthcare system initially
4. **Medical Certificate Approach**:
    - Have your US doctor write a certificate stating you're in "good general health"
    - Mention that you have a well-managed chronic condition
    - Emphasize stability and ongoing successful treatment

**Key Point About Portugal Specifically:** Portugal WANTS people like you - financially independent individuals who will contribute to the economy. Your Crohn's, especially since it's well-controlled, is unlikely to be seen as a barrier when you're clearly bringing financial resources and won't be dependent on social services.

**Practical Next Steps:**

- Discuss this with your current gastroenterologist
- Get a letter stating your condition is stable and well-managed
- Consider getting an updated colonoscopy/blood work before applying to show current stability
- Include this documentation proactively rather than trying to hide it

Remember, you're applying as someone with €🤫 in assets and passive income well above their requirements. A manageable chronic condition that's not communicable or dangerous simply isn't the kind of health issue they're trying to screen out.
