## **User Question 8:**

> We're trying to avoid a mortgage. We would be selling our house worth about 800-900k usd. Would we be able to conntinue paying into our US 401k, or would there be something more sensible/safer/better?
### Claude's Response:

With over $800k, you're in an excellent position to buy outright and avoid mortgage complexities! However, the 401(k) situation is more nuanced than you might expect.

**The 401(k) Challenge:** Once you become a Portuguese tax resident, you can keep your 401(k) but cannot make new contributions because you're no longer employed in the US. The bigger issue is taxation of withdrawals.

**Portugal Tax Implications:**

- The original NHR tax regime is no longer available (deadline passed March 31, 2025)
- However, Portugal now has "NHR 2.0" (IFICI) which offers a 20% flat tax rate on qualifying income
- Under the old NHR, 401(k) distributions were taxed at just 10%, but the new system's treatment is still being clarified
- Retirement income may be tax-exempt in Portugal if taxes are paid in the source country and there's a Double Taxation Agreement

**Better Alternatives to Consider:**

**For Portugal/France:**

1. **Roth IRA conversions** before you move - pay US taxes now, then withdrawals are tax-free
2. **European investment platforms** once resident (lower ongoing fees, EU regulatory protection)
3. **Property investment** - your gîte business could be both income and retirement vehicle
4. **International pension/insurance products** designed for expats

**Immediate Priority:** You absolutely need a US expat tax specialist and a Portuguese tax advisor to model different scenarios before you move. The interplay between US and Portuguese taxation on retirement accounts is complex and costly mistakes are easy to make.

**Strategic Timing:** Consider doing major 401(k) planning (conversions, withdrawals) in your final US tax year to optimize the transition.
