> We have a dog that we want to take with us to France. How do we make that as easy as possible? She would not be good in the hold, but she is crate trained.

Great news - there are definitely ways to make this easier for your dog! Here's the best approach:

**Eurotunnel is Your Best Option:** The Eurotunnel is recommended as the best stress-free way for your dog to travel, and your pet does not have to travel in a crate on Eurotunnel - they can stay with you in your car for the entire 35-minute journey.

**Why Eurotunnel Beats Flying:**

- Dogs aren't allowed to travel in the cabin on UK flights unless they are registered assistance dogs
- Your dog stays with you the entire time - no separation anxiety
- No crate required (though you can use one if she prefers it)
- Much less stressful than cargo hold
- Drive directly to your new home without additional transfers

**Required Documentation (Post-Brexit):** Dogs now need an Animal Health Certificate to travel to France, issued by a vet who will verify rabies vaccination and microchipping. Key requirements:

- You can no longer use a pet passport issued in Great Britain to enter the EU
- You need to depart no more than 10 days after the Animal Health Certificate was issued
- Microchip (essential)
- Up-to-date rabies vaccination
- Tapeworm treatment (24-120 hours before travel for France)

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Visit vet 1-4 weeks before travel** for health certificate
2. **Book Eurotunnel** in advance (gets busy, especially summer)
3. **Arrive 30 minutes early** for pet document checks
4. **Drive onto train with your dog** - she can sit normally or in her crate if preferred
5. **Stay in your car** for the 35-minute crossing
6. **Drive off in France** - no additional stress

**Pro Tips:**

- Book a pet-friendly hotel for your first night to help her adjust
- Bring familiar bedding and toys
- Consider a practice run to the Eurotunnel terminal beforehand
- Some dogs actually enjoy car travel more than flying

**Alternative Airlines (if you must fly):** If Eurotunnel isn't practical, some European airlines allow pets up to 8-10kg in cabin carriers no larger than 40 x 20 x 24cm, but this depends on your dog's size.

What size/breed is your dog? If she's small enough for airline cabin travel, that opens up direct flights to southern France, but Eurotunnel is definitely the most stress-free option for both of you.
