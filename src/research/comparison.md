## Portugal D7 Visa - Complete Process

### **What is the D7 Visa?**

The D7 "Passive Income" visa is designed for non-EU citizens (including post-Brexit UK citizens) who have sufficient income to support themselves without working in Portugal. Perfect for retirees, remote workers, and people with investment income.

### **Income Requirements**

- **Primary applicant**: €870/month (minimum wage equivalent)
- **Spouse**: Additional 50% (€435/month)
- **Total for couple**: €1,305/month (€15,660/year)
- Your $🤫 savings easily covers this requirement

### **Acceptable Income Sources**

- ✅ Investment income/dividends
- ✅ Rental income (including your future gîte)
- ✅ Pension income
- ✅ Bank account interest
- ✅ Savings account statements showing sustainable withdrawals
- ❌ Employment income from Portuguese employer (different visa needed)

### **Required Documentation**

1. **Financial Proof**:
    
    - Bank statements for 3-6 months showing required income
    - Investment account statements
    - Proof of pension income
    - Tax returns from last 2 years
2. **Personal Documents**:
    
    - Valid UK passports
    - Birth certificates (apostilled)
    - Marriage certificate (apostilled)
    - Clean criminal background checks (FBI + UK)
    - Medical insurance covering Portugal (€30k minimum)
    - Medical certificates proving good health
3. **Property/Accommodation**:
    
    - Property purchase agreement, OR
    - Rental contract for minimum 1 year, OR
    - Hotel booking for initial period + proof you're actively property hunting

### **Step-by-Step Application Process**

**Phase 1: Preparation (2-4 months)**

1. **Gather documents** - Start with criminal background checks (longest wait times)
2. **Get documents apostilled** (legalized for international use)
3. **Secure accommodation** - Either buy property or sign rental contract
4. **Get health insurance** covering Portugal (companies like Cigna Global work)
5. **Open Portuguese bank account** (some banks allow this remotely)

**Phase 2: Application (1-2 months)** 6. **Submit application** at Portuguese consulate in Boston or UK 7. **Pay fees**: ~€90 application fee 8. **Attend biometrics appointment** 9. **Wait for approval**: typically 30-90 days

**Phase 3: Moving & Residence (4 months from visa approval)** 10. **Enter Portugal within 4 months** of visa approval 11. **Apply for residence permit** at SEF (immigration service) within first few days 12. **Get Portuguese tax number (NIF)** 13. **Register with local authorities**

### **Timeline to Citizenship**

- **Year 1-5**: Temporary residence permits (renewed annually)
- **Year 5**: Eligible for permanent residence
- **Year 6**: Eligible for Portuguese citizenship (requires basic Portuguese language test)
- **Portuguese passport = Full EU citizenship rights**

## France Visa Options

### **Option 1: Visitor Visa (Visa de Long Séjour Temporaire - VLS-T)**

**Purpose**: For people with passive income who want to live in France without working

**Income Requirements**:

- **Much higher than Portugal**: €1,500+ per month per person minimum
- **Total for couple**: ~€3,000/month (€36,000/year)
- Must prove income will continue for duration of stay
- More scrutinized than Portugal's requirements

**Duration & Renewal**:

- Initial visa: 6-12 months
- Can be renewed for up to 4 years total
- **No automatic path to permanent residence**
- Must eventually switch to different visa category

**Required Documentation**:

- Similar to Portugal but MORE extensive financial proof
- Detailed bank statements for 6+ months
- Proof of accommodation (purchase or long-term rental)
- Comprehensive health insurance
- Criminal background checks (apostilled)
- Proof you won't work in France

**Application Process**:

- Apply at French consulate in US or UK
- Longer processing times: 2-4 months
- More bureaucratic, requires French translations
- Higher rejection rates than Portugal D7

### **Option 2: Long Stay Business Visa (Commerçant)**

**Purpose**: For people establishing a business in France (your gîte operation)

**Requirements**:

- **Detailed business plan** for gîte operation
- **Significant investment**: €30k+ in business
- **Projected income**: Must show business will generate living wage
- **Business registration** in France
- **Professional experience** in hospitality/tourism (helpful)

**Financial Requirements**:

- **Personal funds**: €15k+ per person available funds
- **Business investment**: €30-50k for gîte setup
- **Monthly income projection**: €2,500+ per month from business

**Duration & Path to Residence**:

- Initial period: 1 year
- Renewable if business is successful
- **Can lead to permanent residence** after 5 years
- **Path to citizenship** after 5-10 years (depending on integration)

**Complex Requirements**:

- Must register business BEFORE getting visa
- Requires French business bank account
- Detailed financial projections and market analysis
- Proof of business premises (your gîte property)
- May require French language skills
- Annual reporting requirements

### **Option 3: Skills and Talents Visa (Passeport Talent)**

**Purpose**: For entrepreneurs and investors making significant economic contributions

**Requirements**:

- **Higher investment thresholds**: €300k+ investment
- **Job creation**: Commitment to employ French workers
- **Innovative business model**
- **Strong business experience**

**Benefits**:

- **Fast track to permanent residence**: 4 years instead of 5
- **Multi-entry visa**
- **Family members included**
- **Path to citizenship**

**Realistic Assessment**: This might be overkill for a small gîte operation unless you're planning significant expansion
